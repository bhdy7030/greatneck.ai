GREAT NECK AREA VILLAGES — PERMIT AND INSPECTION PROCEDURES
============================================================
Knowledge Document for RAG Retrieval
Last Updated: 2026-03-04
Applies To: Village of Great Neck, Great Neck Estates, Great Neck Plaza, Kensington, Kings Point, Thomaston
Jurisdiction: Nassau County, New York — Town of North Hempstead
Governing Code: NYS Uniform Fire Prevention and Building Code (19 NYCRR Parts 1202–1203)


SECTION 1: NEW YORK STATE MINIMUM INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS (19 NYCRR 1203.3)
=============================================================================

All six Great Neck area villages must comply with the NYS Uniform Fire Prevention and Building Code.
The minimum standards are set in 19 NYCRR Part 1203, administered by the NYS Department of State
Division of Building Standards and Codes.

1.1 Required Construction Inspections Under NYS Code
-----------------------------------------------------
Per 19 NYCRR 1203.3(b), local jurisdictions MUST conduct inspections at the following stages:

  - Worksite inspection prior to permit issuance
  - Footing and foundation inspection
  - Preparation for concrete slab
  - Framing / structural inspection
  - Rough installation of electrical systems
  - Rough installation of plumbing systems
  - Rough installation of mechanical systems (HVAC)
  - Rough installation of fire protection systems (sprinklers, if applicable)
  - Fire-resistant construction elements
  - Energy Code compliance elements (insulation, air sealing)
  - Final inspection upon project completion

After each inspection, the authority must either:
  (a) Approve the work as satisfactory, OR
  (b) Notify the permit holder of non-compliance, citing the specific code provision violated.

Non-compliant work must remain exposed and accessible until corrected and re-inspected.

1.2 Building Permit Requirements Under NYS Code
------------------------------------------------
Per 19 NYCRR 1203.3(a), permits are required for all work conforming to the Uniform Code.

Exemptions allowed (at local discretion):
  - Detached accessory structures under 144 square feet
  - Certain minor repair work

Permit applications must include:
  - Property identification
  - Occupancy classification
  - Construction documents drawn to scale
  - Documentation prepared by licensed professionals showing code compliance

Permits must:
  - Have specific expiration dates
  - Be visibly displayed at the worksite throughout the project

1.3 Certificate of Occupancy (CO) Requirements Under NYS Code
--------------------------------------------------------------
Per 19 NYCRR 1203.3(d), a CO cannot be issued until:
  - The structure has been inspected
  - Determined to be in compliance with all applicable provisions of the Codes
  - All special inspection reports have been reviewed
  - Flood certifications obtained (if applicable)

CO documents must contain:
  - Permit number
  - Issuance date
  - Property identification
  - Occupancy classification
  - Construction type
  - Special conditions imposed

Temporary COs may be issued when:
  - The structure or portions thereof may be occupied safely
  - Fire safety systems are operational
  - Means of egress are provided


SECTION 2: COMMON PERMIT TYPES ACROSS GREAT NECK VILLAGES
==========================================================

All six villages require permits for the following categories of work:

2.1 Building Permit (General Construction)
------------------------------------------
Required for: New construction, additions, alterations, renovations, structural modifications
Triggers: Any excavation, construction, erection, alteration, or repair of a building or structure
Documents required:
  - Completed building permit application form
  - Architectural drawings and site plans prepared by a NYS-licensed Architect or Engineer
  - Property survey by a NYS-licensed Land Surveyor (if project affects footprint, lot coverage, or setbacks)
  - Contractor license (Nassau County or Town of North Hempstead Home Improvement License)
  - Contractor liability insurance certificate
  - Workers' compensation insurance certificate
  - Disability insurance certificate
  - Village must be listed as certificate holder on insurance documents
Inspections: Multiple (see Section 4)

2.2 Plumbing Permit
--------------------
Required for: Any plumbing installation, modification, or repair
Documents required:
  - Plumbing permit application
  - Description of proposed plumbing work
  - Licensed plumber's name, license number, and insurance
  - Plans showing scope of work
Inspections: Rough-in + Final (see Section 4)

2.3 Electrical Permit
---------------------
Required for: Any electrical installation, modification, or repair
Documents required:
  - Electrical permit application
  - Licensed electrician's name, license number, and insurance
  - Plans/description of electrical work
Inspections: Rough-in + Final (see Section 4)

2.4 Mechanical Permit (HVAC)
-----------------------------
Required for: Heating, ventilating, air conditioning installations or modifications
Documents required:
  - Mechanical permit application
  - Equipment specifications
  - Licensed contractor information and insurance
Inspections: Rough-in + Final

2.5 Demolition Permit
----------------------
Required for: Full or partial demolition of structures
Documents required:
  - Demolition permit application
  - Asbestos survey (required by NYS for pre-1981 buildings)
  - Site plan showing what is being demolished
  - Insurance documentation
Note: Kings Point charges $10,000 for demolition of substantially all non-residential structures.

2.6 Swimming Pool Permit
-------------------------
Required for: In-ground and above-ground pools
Requirements:
  - Pool must be located entirely in the rear yard
  - Cannot exceed 25% of unoccupied rear yard area (Great Neck Estates)
  - Minimum 20 feet from any lot line (Great Neck Estates)
  - Must be enclosed by security fence minimum 4 feet high with self-closing/self-latching gate
  - Location survey required
  - Kings Point pool permit fee: $1,500
  - Great Neck Estates pool permit fee: $300
Inspections: Excavation, electrical, plumbing, barrier/fence, final

2.7 Fence Permit
-----------------
Required for: Installation of any fence
Typical height restrictions (varies by village, verify locally):
  - Front yard: Maximum 4 feet
  - Side yard: Maximum 5 feet
  - Rear yard: Maximum 6 feet
Documents required:
  - Permit application
  - Site plan showing fence location, height, and materials
  - Survey showing property lines
  - Contractor insurance (if applicable)
Inspections: Usually single final inspection

2.8 Driveway / Paving Permit
-----------------------------
Required for: Construction, reconstruction, or modification of driveways and parking areas
Additional requirements:
  - If curb cut is needed: separate curb cut permit from the relevant highway authority
    (Town of North Hempstead or Nassau County DPW for county roads)
  - Site plan showing existing and proposed driveway dimensions
  - Drainage plan (to prevent runoff onto neighboring properties or public right-of-way)
Inspections: Subgrade preparation, final paving inspection

2.9 Deck Permit
----------------
Required for: Attached or detached decks
Documents required:
  - Architectural plans showing dimensions, height, railing details
  - Structural details (post footings, beam sizes, joist spacing)
  - Survey showing setback compliance
Inspections: Footing/foundation, framing, final

2.10 Generator Permit
---------------------
Required for: Standby or permanent generator installation
Requirements:
  - Generator Permit Application
  - Sketch plan showing proposed location on property
  - Must comply with zoning setback regulations
  - Must comply with NFPA and NEC standards
  - Contractor names and insurance information
  - Fee: $200 (Great Neck Estates)
Inspections: Electrical, gas connection (if applicable), final

2.11 Tree Removal Permit
--------------------------
Required in most villages for removal of trees above a certain diameter (e.g., 6+ inches in Great Neck Estates)
Kings Point: Approved permits expire six months from date of issuance; no work under expired permit

2.12 Roofing / Siding Permit
------------------------------
Required for: Re-roofing or residing a structure
Documents: Permit application, contractor license and insurance, scope of work description
Inspections: Usually single final inspection (may include mid-work inspection for structural sheathing)

2.13 Temporary Storage Unit Permit
------------------------------------
Required for: Placement of dumpsters or temporary storage containers during construction
Available at: Village of Great Neck and others


SECTION 3: TYPICAL PERMIT PROCESS FLOW
=======================================

3.1 Standard Process (All Villages)
------------------------------------

Step 1 — Pre-Application (Optional but Recommended)
  - Contact village building department to discuss project scope
  - Determine which permits are needed
  - Identify if variances or Planning Board / Architectural Review Board approval is required

Step 2 — Prepare Application Package
  - Hire NYS-licensed Architect or Engineer to prepare construction documents
  - Obtain current property survey from NYS-licensed Land Surveyor
  - Gather contractor licenses and insurance certificates
  - Complete permit application form(s)
  - Pay application/permit fees

Step 3 — Submit Application
  - Submit to village Building Inspector / Building Department
  - Great Neck Estates: "in such form and number of copies as may be prescribed"
  - Village of Great Neck: One original + three copies of drawings, plus PDF on DVD for Planning Board
  - Ensure all documents are complete — incomplete applications will not be accepted (Kings Point explicitly states this)

Step 4 — Plan Review
  - Building Inspector reviews for code compliance
  - Zoning compliance verified (setbacks, lot coverage, FAR, height)
  - If variances needed: application goes to Zoning Board of Appeals (ZBA)
  - If Architectural Review Board (ARB) review required: Kensington requires ARB review for all structures, alterations, and demolitions
  - Kings Point: "Building Permits are issued conditionally pending compliance with Building Department comments noted on the drawings in red"

Step 5 — Permit Issuance
  - Permit issued after successful plan review
  - Post permit at worksite in a place available for public inspection
  - Kings Point: permits valid for 24 months; extension fees required after expiration

Step 6 — Construction with Inspections
  - Contractor schedules inspections with Building Department
  - Inspection requests must be made 24 hours in advance
  - Work must remain accessible and exposed until inspected and accepted
  - Failed inspections: correct work, request re-inspection

Step 7 — Final Inspection and CO/CC
  - Request final inspection upon completion
  - All work must match approved plans
  - Great Neck Estates (structures >2,000 sq ft): Architect/engineer must file a final certificate certifying conformity with filed plans
  - If building footprint changed: as-built survey required before CO issued
  - Sewer district compliance certificate may be required
  - Certificate of Occupancy (CO) or Certificate of Completion (CC) issued

3.2 Typical Review and Approval Timelines
------------------------------------------
NOTE: Timelines vary significantly by village, project complexity, and season.
These are general estimates based on common practice in Nassau County villages.

  - Simple permits (fence, roofing, siding): 1–5 business days
  - Standard building permits (interior renovation): 2–4 weeks
  - Complex projects (additions, new construction): 4–8 weeks
  - Projects requiring variances: Add 4–8 weeks (monthly ZBA meetings)
  - Projects requiring Planning Board review: Monthly meeting cycle
    (Great Neck Planning Board meets third Thursday of each month)
  - Inspection scheduling: 24–48 hours advance notice required
  - Permits expire: December 31 each year (Village of Great Neck) or 24 months from issuance (Kings Point)


SECTION 4: INSPECTION STAGES BY PROJECT TYPE
==============================================

4.1 New Construction / Major Addition
--------------------------------------
Inspection sequence (multiple inspections required):
  1. Pre-construction site inspection (verify survey stakes, site conditions)
  2. Footing / Foundation inspection — before concrete pour
  3. Foundation walls and damp-proofing — before backfill
  4. Underground plumbing — before slab pour or backfill
  5. Concrete slab preparation — before pour
  6. Rough framing — after framing complete, before insulation
     Includes: structural connections, fireblocking, draft-stopping
  7. Rough plumbing — after rough-in, before walls closed
     Includes: supply lines, drain/waste/vent (DWV), pressure test
  8. Rough electrical — after rough-in, before walls closed
     Includes: wiring, boxes, panel, grounding
  9. Rough mechanical (HVAC) — after rough-in, before walls closed
     Includes: ductwork, refrigerant lines, gas piping
  10. Insulation / Energy Code inspection — before drywall
      Includes: R-values, air sealing, vapor barriers per NYS Energy Code
  11. Fire-resistant construction (if applicable) — firewall ratings, draft stops
  12. Final plumbing — fixtures installed, water test
  13. Final electrical — devices, fixtures, panel schedule, GFCI/AFCI testing
  14. Final mechanical — equipment startup, ventilation verification
  15. Final building inspection — overall completion, egress, safety
  16. Certificate of Occupancy issued upon passing all finals

4.2 Interior Renovation / Alteration
-------------------------------------
Inspections typically required:
  1. Rough framing (if structural changes)
  2. Rough plumbing (if plumbing work)
  3. Rough electrical (if electrical work)
  4. Rough mechanical (if HVAC work)
  5. Insulation (if exterior walls opened or Energy Code triggered)
  6. Final inspection
  7. Certificate of Completion issued

4.3 Plumbing-Only Project
--------------------------
Inspections:
  1. Underground rough (if below slab or underground): before backfill/cover
  2. Rough-in inspection: supply and DWV piping installed, pressure tested, before walls closed
  3. Final inspection: fixtures connected, tested, operational

4.4 Electrical-Only Project
-----------------------------
Inspections:
  1. Rough-in inspection: wiring, boxes, panel connections — before walls closed
  2. Final inspection: devices installed, circuits tested, GFCI/AFCI verified

4.5 Structural / Framing
--------------------------
Inspections:
  1. Footing inspection: before concrete pour, verify dimensions, rebar, soil bearing
  2. Foundation inspection: foundation walls complete, before backfill
  3. Rough framing: all structural members in place, connections verified, before closing walls
     Checked items: beam sizes, joist spacing, header sizes, hold-downs, hurricane ties,
     sheathing nailing patterns, fireblocking

4.6 Foundation Work
--------------------
Inspections:
  1. Footing excavation: soil conditions, dimensions, rebar placement
  2. Footing pour: before concrete placement
  3. Foundation walls: formwork, rebar, anchor bolts — before pour
  4. Waterproofing/damp-proofing: before backfill
  5. Backfill: final grade around foundation

4.7 Driveway / Paving
-----------------------
Inspections:
  1. Subgrade preparation: base material, compaction, drainage provisions
  2. Final paving inspection: surface grade, drainage, compliance with approved plans

4.8 Deck Construction
----------------------
Inspections:
  1. Footing holes: depth, diameter, soil conditions — before concrete
  2. Framing: posts, beams, joists, ledger attachment, hardware — before decking
  3. Final: decking, railings (36" min for residential, 42" for commercial), stairs, handrails

4.9 Fence Installation
-----------------------
Inspections:
  Single final inspection: height, location relative to property lines, materials match approval

4.10 Roofing
--------------
Inspections:
  Single or two inspections depending on scope:
  - Sheathing inspection (if structural sheathing replaced)
  - Final inspection: materials, flashing, ventilation

4.11 Generator Installation
-----------------------------
Inspections:
  1. Electrical inspection: transfer switch, wiring, grounding
  2. Gas connection inspection (if natural gas): gas piping, pressure test
  3. Final: operational test, setback verification, noise compliance

SECTION 6: REQUIRED DOCUMENTS SUMMARY
=======================================

6.1 For All Building Permit Applications
------------------------------------------
  - Completed permit application form (village-specific)
  - Architectural drawings/construction documents (prepared by NYS-licensed Architect or Engineer)
    - Must be drawn to scale
    - Include floor plans, elevations, sections, structural details
  - Property survey (NYS-licensed Land Surveyor) — required when:
    - New construction
    - Additions that change building footprint
    - Projects affecting lot coverage or setbacks
    - As-built survey required for CO when footprint changed
  - Contractor documentation:
    - Nassau County or Town of North Hempstead Home Improvement Contractor License
    - Licensed Master Plumber (for plumbing work)
    - Licensed Master Electrician (for electrical work)
    - General liability insurance certificate
    - Workers' compensation insurance certificate
    - Disability insurance certificate
    - Village listed as certificate holder
  - Permit fee payment

6.2 Additional Documents by Project Type
------------------------------------------
  - Tree plan (Great Neck Estates: showing all trees within 50 feet)
  - Drainage plan (Kings Point: for all construction projects)
  - Asbestos survey (demolition of pre-1981 structures)
  - Sewer district compliance certificate (Great Neck Estates: before CO)
  - Flood zone documentation (if applicable)
  - Energy Code compliance documentation
  - Special inspection reports (from third-party inspectors, if required)
  - Architect/engineer final conformity certificate (Great Neck Estates: for structures >2,000 sq ft)

6.3 For Variance Applications
-------------------------------
  - All standard permit documents
  - Radius map (typically 200 feet from property boundaries)
  - Certified mailing labels for all property owners within radius
  - Environmental assessment form
  - Statement of hardship or practical difficulty

SECTION 8: IMPORTANT NOTES FOR RAG AGENT
==========================================

8.1 Always Recommend Calling the Village
------------------------------------------
Permit requirements, fees, and procedures can change. Always advise users to
contact their specific village building department to confirm current requirements
before starting work.

8.2 Village vs. Town vs. County Jurisdiction
---------------------------------------------
The Great Neck area has overlapping jurisdictions:
  - VILLAGE building department: handles building permits, inspections, COs
  - TOWN of North Hempstead: handles some licensing, solid waste, certain zoning matters
  - NASSAU COUNTY: handles county road permits, fire marshal, some licensing
  - NEW YORK STATE: sets minimum building code standards that all villages must meet
Users should always start with their VILLAGE building department for construction permits.

8.3 Shared Personnel
----------------------
Some building officials serve multiple villages:
  - Mark Vincent Kruse, AIA serves both Kensington and Thomaston
  - Michael Sweeney serves Village of Great Neck and Great Neck Estates
This is common in small Nassau County villages. Office hours and response times may be affected.

8.4 Insurance and Licensing Requirements Are Strict
-----------------------------------------------------
All villages require valid, current contractor licensing and insurance.
Kings Point explicitly states that expired or invalid documents are discarded without notification.
The village must typically be listed as certificate holder on insurance documents.
Homeowners should verify their contractor's credentials before hiring.

8.5 Permit Expiration
-----------------------
Permits have expiration dates:
  - Village of Great Neck: ALL permits expire December 31 each year
  - Kings Point: Permits valid for 24 months, extension fees apply after
  - Other villages: Varies — check with village
Expired permits may require reapplication and additional fees.
